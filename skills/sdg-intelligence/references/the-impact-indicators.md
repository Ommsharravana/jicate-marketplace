# THE Impact Rankings -- Per-SDG Indicators (2025)

> Source: Times Higher Education Impact Rankings 2025
> Published: June 2025 | Methodology Version 1.2
> Participants: 2,152+ universities globally
> Developed in conjunction with Vertigo Ventures (Hivve) and Elsevier

---

## Overview

The THE Impact Rankings are the only global performance tables that assess universities against all 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals. Universities are assessed across four broad pillars:

1. **Research** -- Creating research in relevant SDG topics (Elsevier bibliometric data, 5-year window 2019-2023)
2. **Stewardship** -- How universities manage their resources, employees, faculty and students
3. **Outreach** -- Work with local, regional, national and international communities
4. **Teaching** -- Ensuring skilled practitioners and sustainable alumni

---

## Overall Ranking Methodology

- Universities submit data on SDG 17 (mandatory) plus at least 3 other SDGs
- Overall score = SDG 17 score (22%) + best 3 other SDG scores (26% each)
- Scores are scaled so highest = 100, lowest = 0 within each SDG
- Overall ranking averages the past two years' total scores
- No minimum research requirement for participation

---

## Metric Categories

1. **Research metrics** -- Elsevier bibliometric data with SDG-specific queries, supplemented by AI-identified publications
2. **Continuous metrics** -- Quantitative measures that vary across a range, usually size-normalized
3. **Evidence metrics** -- Policies and initiatives requiring documented evidence; credit for evidence being public

## Research Metrics -- Common Across All SDGs

Each SDG has research contributing 27% of its score (except SDG 9 at 11.6%). The research component includes:

- **Publication volume** -- Number of relevant publications identified by SDG-specific Scopus queries
- **Citation impact** -- Field-weighted citation impact of SDG-relevant research
- **AI-supplemented identification** -- Machine learning identifies additional relevant papers beyond keyword queries
- **Time window** -- 5-year rolling window (2019-2023 for 2025 edition)

---

## Evidence Requirements

When THE asks about policies and initiatives:
- Universities must provide documented evidence
- Credit is given for the evidence existing AND for the evidence being publicly available
- Evidence is evaluated against specific criteria
- Decisions are cross-validated where uncertainty exists
- Evidence need not be exhaustive -- examples demonstrating best practice suffice

---

## Data Reference Period

- Academic year closest to January-December 2023
- Research data: 2019-2023 (5-year window)
- Exception: SDG 9 patents relate to patent publication timeframe, not research timeframe
