# NIRF SDG Rankings -- 2025 Parameters

> Source: National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF), Ministry of Education, India
> Launched: 2025 (debut edition). 791 institutions participated.
> IIT Madras topped the inaugural SDG rankings.

---

## Overview

NIRF 2025 introduced the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) category as its 17th ranking category, expanding from the 16 categories in 2024. This aligns Indian higher education evaluation with the UN 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

The SDG category evaluates how institutions contribute to environmental sustainability, gender equity, community development, and inclusivity through both operational practices and research output.

---

## NIRF Core Parameters (Applied Across All Categories)

| Parameter | Weight (Overall) | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Teaching, Learning & Resources (TLR) | 30% | Faculty quality, infrastructure, academic resources, NEP 2020-aligned practices |
| Research & Professional Practice (RP) | 30% | Publications, citations, patents, funded projects, consultancy |
| Graduation Outcomes (GO) | 20% | Exam performance, PhD output, placements (discipline-specific) |
| Outreach & Inclusivity (OI) | 10% | Diversity, equity, accessibility, regional representation |
| Perception (PR) | 10% | Employer and academic peer reputation, accreditation status |

## SDG-Specific Evaluation Areas

The SDG ranking category evaluates institutions on contributions to UN Sustainable Development Goals through these dimensions:

### 1. Clean Energy Contributions
- Renewable energy installations on campus (solar, wind)
- Energy efficiency programs and targets
- Research output on clean and affordable energy (SDG 7)
- Clean energy technology development and transfer

### 2. Climate Action Research and Initiatives
- Climate-related research publications
- Carbon footprint measurement and reduction programs
- Net-zero commitment and progress
- Climate adaptation and resilience programs
- Environmental education curriculum integration

### 3. Inclusive Education Programs
- Programs for first-generation learners
- Scholarships and financial aid for disadvantaged students
- Accessibility infrastructure for persons with disabilities
- Multiple entry/exit options (NEP 2020)
- Regional language course offerings
- Open educational resources and MOOCs

### 4. Gender Equality Measures
- Women student enrollment ratios
- Women faculty representation
- Women in leadership positions
- Gender sensitization programs
- Anti-sexual harassment mechanisms
- Support systems for women researchers

### 5. Sustainable Cities and Communities Contributions
- Community outreach and extension activities
- Urban planning and sustainable development research
- Heritage preservation initiatives
- Sustainable campus operations
- Public transport and green mobility

### 6. Sustainable Living Practices (MIRS/MIR Sub-parameter)
- Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) integration
- Sustainable living curriculum elements
- Campus sustainability operations
- Green audit and compliance
- Waste management and recycling programs

## Research Parameter -- PSDGs (Publications in SDGs)

### What It Is
A new sub-parameter under Research & Professional Practice (RP) that evaluates research output specifically mapped to UN Sustainable Development Goals.

### How It Works
- Publications are mapped to SDG keywords using Scopus/Web of Science classification
- Five-year publication window (rolling)
- Citations from SDG-mapped publications counted separately
- Quality metrics (h-index, journal impact) applied to SDG research
- Self-citations excluded

### SDG Research Mapping Categories
| Research Area | Primary SDGs |
|---------------|-------------|
| Poverty and inequality research | SDG 1, 10 |
| Food security and agriculture | SDG 2 |
| Health sciences and public health | SDG 3 |
| Education and pedagogy | SDG 4 |
| Gender studies | SDG 5 |
| Water resources and sanitation | SDG 6 |
| Energy research | SDG 7 |
| Economics and employment | SDG 8 |
| Engineering and innovation | SDG 9 |
| Urban planning and sustainability | SDG 11 |
| Environmental sciences | SDG 12, 13, 14, 15 |
| Law, governance, and policy | SDG 16 |
| International cooperation | SDG 17 |

## Research Integrity Reforms (2025)

NIRF 2025 introduced negative weightage for retracted research papers:
- Formula devised to penalize based on number/percentage of retracted publications
- Penalty is minor in 2025 but will increase in future editions
- Self-citations excluded from citation metrics
- Data transparency: institutions must publish submitted data on websites for 3 years

## Sustainability Integration in TLR Parameter

The MIRS/MIR sub-parameter within TLR evaluates:
- Multiple Entry/Exit program adoption
- Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) course offerings
- Regional language instruction availability
- Sustainable living practices integration in curriculum
- NEP 2020 alignment indicators

## SDG Evidence Types for NIRF

| Evidence Category | Metrics | Relevant SDGs |
|-------------------|---------|---------------|
| Energy | Solar capacity (kW), LED adoption %, energy consumption per student | SDG 7, 13 |
| Water | Rainwater harvesting capacity, water recycled %, per-student consumption | SDG 6 |
| Waste | Recycling rate %, composting %, e-waste disposed properly | SDG 12 |
| Green Cover | Trees planted, green area %, biodiversity index | SDG 15 |
| Carbon | Carbon footprint (tCO2e), reduction trajectory, net-zero timeline | SDG 13 |
| Research | PSDGs count, SDG-mapped citations, SDG-focused funded projects | All 17 |
| Inclusion | Women enrollment %, PwD enrollment %, SC/ST/OBC representation | SDG 5, 10 |
| Community | Extension activities count, NSS/NCC participation, outreach programs | SDG 1, 2, 3, 11 |
| Innovation | Student startups, patents with SDG relevance, technology transfer | SDG 8, 9 |
| Governance | Green policy adoption, sustainability committee, annual reporting | SDG 16, 17 |

## Category-Specific Weight Adjustments

The SDG category may adjust standard NIRF weights to emphasize sustainability:
- Research parameter likely weights PSDGs more heavily than standard RP
- Outreach & Inclusivity parameter may weight community sustainability higher
- TLR parameter emphasizes sustainable living and IKS components
- Graduation Outcomes may track alumni in sustainability sectors

Note: Exact SDG-category-specific weights have not been publicly disclosed as of March 2026. The above is based on available NIRF documentation and expert analysis.

## Key Differences: NIRF SDG vs THE Impact Rankings

| Dimension | NIRF SDG | THE Impact Rankings |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|
| Geographic scope | India only | Global |
| Number of institutions | 791 (2025 debut) | 2,152+ (2025) |
| Core methodology | 5-parameter NIRF framework + SDG overlay | Per-SDG bespoke indicators |
| Research data | Scopus/WoS publications | Elsevier bibliometrics |
| Scoring | Composite score | SDG 17 + best 3 other SDGs |
| NEP alignment | Yes (MIRS/MIR parameter) | No |
| Mandatory SDGs | All assessed together | Choose which SDGs to submit |
| Frequency | Annual (since 2025) | Annual (since 2019) |

## Preparation Checklist for Institutions

1. Map all research publications to SDG keywords in Scopus/WoS
2. Document campus sustainability operations with measurable metrics
3. Collect gender and inclusion statistics across all programs
4. Catalog community outreach activities with impact data
5. Prepare green audit reports with year-over-year comparisons
6. Document NEP 2020 implementation (MEE, IKS, regional languages)
7. Track and report carbon footprint with reduction targets
8. Maintain records of sustainability partnerships and collaborations
9. Publish all NIRF-submitted data on institutional website (mandatory)
10. Verify zero retracted publications or document corrective actions
