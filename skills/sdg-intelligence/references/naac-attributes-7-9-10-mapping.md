# NAAC Binary Framework -- Attributes 7, 9 & 10 SDG Mapping

> Source: NAAC Binary Accreditation Framework (2025)
> NAAC moved from 7 Criteria (grading A++/A+/A/B++/B+/B/C) to 10 Attributes (Binary: Accredited / Not Accredited) in 2025.
> Weightage: 25% Input attributes, 75% Process & Output attributes.

## Attribute 7: Governance and Administration

Evaluates the effectiveness of leadership, the governing body, and administrative processes. Ethical leadership and efficient management ensure effective policy implementation.

### Metrics
- Organograms and governance structure documentation
- E-governance systems implementation and usage
- Grievance redressal mechanisms and resolution rates
- Strategic planning and execution evidence
- Decentralization and participative management
- Quality assurance mechanisms (IQAC effectiveness)
- Institutional best practices documentation
- Innovation and distinctiveness evidence
- Professional ethics and code of conduct
- Internal and external audit compliance

### SDG Mapping

| Evidence Type | Primary SDG | Secondary SDGs | Rationale |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| Transparent governance structures | SDG 16 | SDG 17 | Peace, justice and strong institutions |
| E-governance adoption | SDG 9 | SDG 16 | Innovation in institutional operations |
| Grievance redressal & anti-discrimination | SDG 16 | SDG 5, 10 | Justice, gender equality, reduced inequalities |
| Ethics and code of conduct | SDG 16 | SDG 4 | Strong institutions, quality education |
| Participative management | SDG 16 | SDG 17 | Inclusive decision-making, partnerships |
| Quality assurance (IQAC) | SDG 4 | SDG 17 | Quality education, institutional partnerships |
| Best practices documentation | SDG 4 | SDG 17 | Education quality improvement, knowledge sharing |
| Strategic planning for sustainability | SDG 11 | SDG 13 | Sustainable communities, climate action planning |
| Financial transparency and audits | SDG 16 | SDG 8 | Accountable institutions, economic governance |
| Gender equity in governance | SDG 5 | SDG 10 | Women in leadership, reduced inequalities |

### Primary SDGs: 4, 5, 9, 10, 16, 17

## Attribute 9: Research and Innovation Outcomes

Focuses on fostering a culture of research, development, and creativity. Evaluates contributions to academic research, publications, patents, and industry partnerships.

### Metrics
- External research grants secured (amount and number)
- Research publications in Scopus/WoS indexed journals
- Research quality (citations, h-index, journal impact factor)
- PhDs awarded (number per year, three-year average)
- Research fellowships received
- IPRs produced (patents published and granted)
- Research collaborations (national and international)
- Number of student startups and spin-offs
- Consultancy revenue
- Technology transfer and licensing

### SDG Mapping

| Evidence Type | Primary SDG | Secondary SDGs | Rationale |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| SDG-mapped research publications (PSDGs) | SDG 9 | All 17 SDGs | Research directly addressing global challenges |

| Clean energy research | SDG 7 | SDG 13, 9 | Affordable energy, climate action |

| Social science and equity research | SDG 10 | SDG 5, 16 | Reducing inequalities through evidence |
| Education research | SDG 4 | SDG 10 | Quality education improvement |

## Attribute 10: Sustainability (Green Initiatives)

As environmental challenges grow, this attribute highlights the importance of green initiatives in HEIs. Evaluates eco-friendly campus designs, waste management, energy conservation, and water-saving practices.

### Metrics
- Green audit conducted and published
- Campus energy consumption and renewable energy adoption (solar capacity, LED adoption)
- Water conservation measures (rainwater harvesting, recycled water usage)
- Waste management programs (composting, recycling rates, e-waste disposal)
- Carbon footprint tracking and reduction targets
- Progress towards net-zero operations
- Green cover and biodiversity measures (trees planted, green area percentage)
- Environmental policies and their implementation evidence
- Collaborations with industry/NGOs for sustainability
- Environmental awareness programs for students and community
- Sustainable procurement policies
- Green building certifications (IGBC, GRIHA, LEED)

## Evidence Collection Strategy for NAAC

### Attribute 7 Evidence with SDG Tags
1. Board of Governance minutes showing sustainability discussions -> SDG 13, 16
2. IQAC minutes with SDG-linked quality improvements -> SDG 4, 17
3. Anti-ragging/anti-discrimination committee reports -> SDG 5, 10, 16
4. Financial audit reports showing sustainability spending -> SDG 8, 16
5. E-governance implementation documentation -> SDG 9, 16

### Attribute 9 Evidence with SDG Tags
1. Scopus publications mapped to SDG keywords -> All relevant SDGs
2. Patent portfolio with SDG classification -> SDG 9 + specific SDGs
3. Funded project sanction letters with SDG alignment -> SDG 9, 17
4. MoU documents with SDG-relevant collaborations -> SDG 17
5. Startup registration certificates with social impact -> SDG 8, 9

### Attribute 10 Evidence with SDG Tags
1. Green audit report (certified) -> SDG 6, 7, 12, 13, 15
2. Energy bills showing reduction trend -> SDG 7, 13
3. Solar installation capacity certificates -> SDG 7
4. Rainwater harvesting capacity documentation -> SDG 6
5. Waste management SOP and recycling data -> SDG 12
6. Tree census and biodiversity audit -> SDG 15
7. Carbon footprint assessment report -> SDG 13
8. Green building certification (IGBC/GRIHA/LEED) -> SDG 11
9. Sustainability partnerships MoUs -> SDG 17
