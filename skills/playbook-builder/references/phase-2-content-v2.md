## Core Principle: 90/10 Rule

- **90% benefits and visualization** — what life looks like AFTER transformation
- **10% credibility signals** — just enough to prove JICATE isn't vaporware (case study numbers, client count)

> "A mattress ad doesn't show the factory. It shows someone waking up refreshed."

## 8-Section Structure

### Section 1: The Wake-Up Call (Hook)

**Purpose:** Create immediate emotional recognition — "this is MY life"

**Content rules:**
- Title pattern: "Is This Your [Day/Week/Month]?"
- 4-5 vivid micro-scenarios the prospect will recognize instantly
- Written in second person present tense: "It's 9 AM. Your receptionist calls in sick..."
- NO stats. NO industry data. Pure recognition moments.
- Each scenario must be specific to the target industry (not generic business problems)
- Ending: "If even ONE of these made you wince, keep reading."

**What makes it work:** The prospect sees themselves. Not a statistic — their actual Tuesday morning.

**Research data used:** Pain Hunter findings — but converted from stats to stories. "13% dropout rate" becomes "A patient you spent 6 months treating just stops showing up. No call. No message. Gone."

### Section 2: The Invisible Tax (Pain Quantified)

**Purpose:** Turn vague frustration into a personalized number

**Content rules:**
- Interactive "What's This Costing You?" calculator
- 4-6 simple input fields (sliders or number inputs) specific to the industry:
  - Industry-specific: chairs/locations/staff/patients/clients (varies by industry)
  - Conversion rate (what % of enquiries become customers?)
  - Missed appointments or no-shows per month
  - Hours spent on manual billing/reconciliation per week
- Output formula: Calculate estimated annual revenue leak in local currency
- The number MUST be personalized to their inputs — never a generic industry stat
- Below calculator: "Most [industry] owners we talk to are shocked by this number. The good news: most of it is recoverable."

**What makes it work:** Their OWN number, not an industry average. Personalized pain hits differently.

**Research data used:** Stat Collector findings — to build realistic multipliers for the calculator (average revenue per chair, cost per missed appointment, hourly cost of manual reconciliation).

### Section 3: "Your Monday, Transformed" (Before/After)

**Purpose:** Make them SEE and FEEL the transformation hour by hour

**Content rules:**
- Side-by-side timeline: "Your [Day] Today" vs "Your [Day] with JICATE"
- 6-8 time slots from morning to evening
- LEFT (rose-tinted): Current reality — specific, painful, recognizable
- RIGHT (teal-tinted): Transformed reality — specific, effortless, delightful
- Each time slot is a mini-story, not a feature description
- The "After" side NEVER mentions technology names, architecture, or features
- The "After" side shows OUTCOMES: "You glance at your phone. ₹2.8L collected today. Zero manual reconciliation."
- Industry-specific activities at each time slot

**What makes it work:** Hour-by-hour comparison is visceral. They can FEEL the difference between their chaotic morning and the calm alternative.

**Research data used:** Pain Hunter + Trend Scanner — real workflow pain points converted to before/after narratives.

### Section 4: "What We See That You Can't" (Intelligence Preview)

**Purpose:** Show the VALUE of AI intelligence without teaching HOW

**Content rules:**
- NOT "here's how AI works" — instead "here's what AI REVEALS about businesses like yours"
- 4-5 intelligence insights that would be impossible without connected data
- Each insight follows the pattern:
  1. **The invisible pattern** — "Your [metric] drops [X]% on [day] — because [non-obvious reason]"
  2. **What it costs you** — in local currency, per month
  3. **What happens when you act on it** — specific outcome
- These insights must be INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC and feel like insider knowledge
- Closing: "These are real patterns we find in businesses like yours. What patterns are hiding in YOUR data?"
- NEVER explain the technology behind the insight. Show the revelation, not the engine.

**What makes it work:** Creates "how did they know that?" curiosity. The prospect realizes they're blind to patterns in their own data — and JICATE can see them.

**Research data used:** All three agents — synthesize real industry patterns into "insights" format.

### Section 5: How It Happens (JICATE's Process)

**Purpose:** Show what JICATE does FOR them — not a DIY roadmap

**Content rules:**
- Three phases, described as JICATE's actions (every sentence starts with "We"):
  - **Week 1-2: "We X-ray your business"** — JICATE maps workflows, data points, bottlenecks. You answer questions. We do the work.
  - **Week 3-6: "We build your nervous system"** — Core modules go live. TallyPrime syncs. Team gets trained on THEIR workflows.
  - **Week 7-12: "We turn on the brain"** — AI layer activates. First natural language query works. Predictions start.
- CRITICAL: The subject of every sentence is "We" (JICATE), not "You" (prospect)
- Ending: "You don't hire an architect and build the house yourself. You hire us. We build it. You move in."
- NO technical details about what modules, what architecture, what integrations
- Pure outcome language: "Your collections reconcile automatically" not "bi-directional TallyPrime sync"

**What makes it work:** Positions JICATE as the builder, not the advisor. The prospect's role is to "move in," not to implement.

### Section 6: Proof (Case Study, Narrative)

**Purpose:** Social proof — someone else did it, it worked, you can too

**Content rules:**
- ONE case study told as a NARRATIVE (not bullet points)
- Use the anonymized case study from `/Users/omm/PROJECTS/jicate-playbooks/reference/jkkn-case-study.md`
- **CRITICAL: Bridge the industry gap explicitly.** The case study is from education, not the target industry. You MUST address this head-on: "They weren't a [target industry] business. But the problems were the same — [list 3 shared pain points]." Never let the reader think "this doesn't apply to me."
- Frame the bridge around UNIVERSAL problems, not industry-specific ones: manual reconciliation, WhatsApp-based operations, multi-location chaos, Excel dependency, zero analytics
- Maximum 3 paragraphs of story + 3 stat boxes
- Stats: Only the most dramatic numbers (93% time reduction, 80% payroll reduction, 19 modules)
- Closing: "They didn't have a bigger team. They didn't have a bigger budget. They had JICATE."
- **If research finds an ACTUAL same-industry case study or reference client — use that instead. Same-industry proof is 5x more credible than cross-industry bridging.**
- For white-label: Replace with "How businesses like yours are transforming" — general success patterns without naming JICATE or the case study institution

### Section 7: "Get Your Free Practice X-Ray" (Primary CTA)

**Purpose:** Convert — move the assessment INTO JICATE's hands

**Content rules:**
- Headline: "Stop Guessing. See Your Business Through AI Eyes."
- Subline: "Give us your website and 60 seconds. We'll show you what we see."
- Form fields:
  - Company name (required)
  - Website URL (required)
  - Your name (required)
  - Your role (required)
  - WhatsApp number (optional)
  - Hidden honeypot field (bot detection)
- Consent checkbox (MANDATORY): "I agree to receive a personalized analysis from [JICATE Solutions / Client Name]"
- Submit button: "Show Me My X-Ray"
- Below form: "No sales pitch. No 90-slide deck. Just a personalized analysis of YOUR business."
- Privacy note: "We only use publicly available information from your website and public reviews."

**What happens after submit:** Form data sent to JICATE (email for V2, n8n webhook later). JICATE runs `/playbook-builder --company` to generate the X-ray. Delivered within 48 hours.

**For white-label:** Client's email/URL replaces JICATE branding.

### Section 8: "One More Thing" (Soft Close)

**Purpose:** For prospects not ready for the X-ray — give a softer next step

**Content rules:**
- "Not ready for the X-ray? That's fine."
- Three options:
  1. "Talk to someone who's done it" — connect with a reference client
  2. "See a 3-minute demo" — link to video (if available) or "coming soon"
  3. "Just bookmark this" — "We're not going anywhere."
- Contact: solutions@jicate.solutions (or white-label equivalent)
- Tone: NO PRESSURE. Anti-sales. "We don't chase. We build."
- For white-label: Client's contact info replaces JICATE

## Content Quality Rules (V2)

| Rule | How to Enforce |
|------|---------------|
| **No technical jargon** | Zero mentions of: API, architecture, module, layer, integration, database, RBAC, SSO, PWA. If it sounds like a developer wrote it, rewrite. |
| **No DIY enablement** | Every section should make the prospect think "I need someone to do this for me" not "I could do this myself" |
| **90/10 ratio** | Count sentences: max 10% can reference JICATE capability or credibility. Rest is pure benefit/emotion. |
| **Second person present tense** | "You open your phone" not "The user opens their phone" — immersive, not clinical |
| **Industry-specific scenarios** | Every story, every time slot, every insight must use real terminology from the target industry. No generic "business owner" language. |
| **Local currency and context** | Use auto-detected currency from `localization.json`. Reference local regulations, payment systems, and business norms. |
| **Real numbers in calculator** | Calculator multipliers must come from research data points, not made up. If a multiplier can't be sourced, use conservative estimates and label as "industry estimate." |

## Banned Phrases (V2 additions to V1 list)

All V1 banned phrases still apply, plus:

- "AI-native operating system" (too technical)
- "4-layer architecture" (gives away the blueprint)
- "module" (developer language)
- "integration" (developer language — use "connects with" or "talks to")
- "dashboard" (use "you can see" or "at a glance")
- "workflow engine" (use "things happen automatically")
- "RBAC" or "role-based access" (use "everyone sees what they need to")
- "PWA" or "progressive web app" (use "works on any phone, no download needed")
- "natural language query" (use "just ask a question in plain English")
